<p><p>The computer chip market is often categorized into two main types: Northbridge and Southbridge, both of which are crucial for linking different components within a computer system. Northbridge manages high-speed communication between the CPU, RAM, and graphics card, facilitating intensive data processes. In contrast, Southbridge handles slower peripherals such as USB, audio, and storage interfaces. Together, they optimize system performance by dividing tasks based on speed and function, creating a balanced architecture in computers.</p></p>
