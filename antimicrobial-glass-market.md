<p><p>Antimicrobial glass incorporates active agents like silver, silver-zinc, silver-copper, or other compounds to inhibit microbial growth. Silver-based glass uses silver ions to disrupt bacterial cell functions. Silver-zinc combines silver's antimicrobial properties with zinc's stability, enhancing durability. Silver-copper leverages copper's natural biocidal effects for broader-spectrum protection. Other types may use alternative agents like titanium dioxide or organic compounds. These glasses are used in healthcare, food processing, and public spaces to reduce contamination, ensuring safer, hygienic surfaces while maintaining transparency and durability.</p></p>
