<p><p>The noninvasive glucose monitor market is segmented into wearable and non-wearable monitors. Wearable monitors, such as patches or smartwatches, continuously track glucose levels through the skin, offering real-time data for managing diabetes. Non-wearable monitors, on the other hand, typically use techniques like infrared spectroscopy or breath analysis to measure glucose levels without skin contact. Both types aim to enhance convenience and accuracy, reducing the reliance on traditional blood sampling methods while improving the overall user experience in glucose management.</p></p>
