<p><p>Metal Oxide Varistors (MOVs) are voltage-dependent resistors that protect circuits from voltage spikes by clamping excessive energy. The market for MOVs is segmented into two main types: SMD (Surface Mount Device) and Leaded types. SMD MOVs are compact and suited for high-density PCB applications, providing efficient protection in smaller devices. Leaded MOVs, with their traditional pins, are used in larger assemblies, offering robust protection for high-power applications. Both types play vital roles in safeguarding electronic systems against transient voltage surges.</p></p>
