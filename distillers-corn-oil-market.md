<p><p>The Distillers Corn Oil market primarily features two processing methods: dry-milling and wet-milling. In dry-milling, corn is processed to produce ethanol, where the byproduct is distillers dried grains with solubles (DDGS) and distillers corn oil. This method is efficient and widely used for biofuel production. In contrast, wet-milling involves soaking corn to separate its components, leading to higher yields of corn oil and other co-products like starch and protein. Both methods cater to diverse industrial applications and market demands.</p></p>
