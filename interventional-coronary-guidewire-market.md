<p><p>The interventional coronary guidewire market comprises two main types: conventional guidewires and specialty guidewires. Conventional guidewires are standard tools used for navigation during coronary interventions, characterized by their flexibility and torque response, suitable for routine procedures. In contrast, specialty guidewires are designed for specific functions or challenging anatomies, offering enhanced features such as varying stiffness, hydrophilic coatings, or specialized tips to facilitate access in complex lesions. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing interventional cardiology outcomes.</p></p>
