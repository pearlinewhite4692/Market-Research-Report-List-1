<p><p>The PSA testing market encompasses various diagnostic methods, primarily the CLIA (Chemiluminescent Immunoassay) and ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) methods. The CLIA method is known for its high sensitivity and rapid results, making it suitable for large-scale screenings. In contrast, the ELISA method is widely used due to its cost-effectiveness and versatility, though it may take longer to process samples. Both methods are essential for detecting prostate-specific antigen levels, aiding in prostate cancer diagnosis and monitoring treatment efficacy.</p></p>
