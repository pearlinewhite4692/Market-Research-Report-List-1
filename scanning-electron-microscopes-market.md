<p><ul><li>Conventional (High Vacuum) Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM)</li><li>Variable Pressure or Low Vacuum Scanning Electron Microscope (LVSEM)</li><li>Cryo-Scanning Electron Microscope (Cryo-SEM)</li><li>Environmental Scanning Electron Microscope (ESEM)</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM) market includes several types. Conventional SEM operates at high vacuum, providing detailed imaging of samples with minimal contamination. Variable Pressure or Low Vacuum SEM allows for analysis of samples with moisture or loose material without extensive preparation. Cryo-SEM preserves samples at low temperatures, making it ideal for biological specimens. Environmental SEM (ESEM) enables imaging in moist or gaseous environments, facilitating the study of samples in their natural state. Other niche SEM technologies further expand the market possibilities.</p></p>
