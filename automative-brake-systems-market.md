<p><p>Automotive brake systems are categorized into mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, electromagnetic, and combined types. Mechanical systems use cables and levers for braking, ideal for smaller vehicles. Hydraulic systems rely on fluid pressure for efficient force transmission, common in passenger cars. Pneumatic systems use compressed air, suitable for heavy vehicles like trucks. Electromagnetic systems employ magnetic fields for braking, often in hybrid or electric vehicles. Combined systems integrate multiple technologies for enhanced performance and safety, catering to advanced automotive needs. Each type serves specific applications based on vehicle requirements.</p></p>
