<p><p>The Joint Fixation Systems market encompasses various types of fixation devices used in orthopedic surgeries to stabilize bones and facilitate healing. Plates are flat devices that provide structural support, while screws secure bone fragments together. Rods are long, cylindrical implants used for spinal stabilization or fracture repair. Pins serve to hold bone fragments in place during healing, and wires are thin, flexible materials used to bind or stabilize bone structures. Together, these systems are essential in orthopedic treatment.</p></p>
